# /feedback

**Purpose:** Strategic tone and framing review. Not grammar — is this landing the way you think it is?

**Input:** A draft message, email, Slack post, or announcement you're about to send.

**Workflow:**
1. Read the draft and assess:
   - **Intent vs. landing:** What you mean to say vs. how it might be read
   - **Tone register:** Is it calibrated for the audience and relationship?
   - **Potential misreads:** What could be taken the wrong way, and why
   - **What's missing:** Context, softening, or specificity that would help it land better
   - **What to cut:** Anything that undermines the main point
2. Give a direct assessment. If it's good, say so — don't pad.
3. Offer one concrete suggestion per issue, not a rewrite.

**Output format:**
```
Overall: [one sentence assessment]

What lands well:
• [point]

Watch for:
• [potential misread or issue + why]

Suggestions:
• [concrete fix — not a rewrite]
```

**Constraints:**
- No em dashes
- Be direct — if something is likely to land badly, say so clearly. Don't soften to uselessness.
- Do NOT rewrite the draft. Offer targeted suggestions only.
- If the draft is clean and well-calibrated, say "This lands well. Ready to send." and stop.
