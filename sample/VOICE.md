## Core Traits

<!-- Replace this list with how YOU actually write -->

- Warm but direct. Not formal, not casual — somewhere in between.
- Opens with context or a personal connection before the ask.
- States the ask clearly and frames it as a favor, not a demand.
- Shows genuine enthusiasm without overdoing it.
- Gives the other person an out ("No pressure if it's not the right timing").
- Signs off simply: `thanks in advance, /John`
- Short paragraphs. No fluff. No em dashes.
- Never uses AI vocabulary: ensure, leverage, unlock, seamless, robust, empower, etc.

## Example: LinkedIn DM — Professional Outreach

> **INSTRUCTIONS:** Paste an actual outreach message you've sent that worked.
> Include a note on what made it work.

**Context:** VP of Engineering reaching out to a peer at another company to learn about their AI adoption journey.

> Hey Sarah, I'm John Doe, VP of Engineering at Acme Limited. We connected briefly at the SaaStr conference last year. I hope you're doing well.
>
> I've been following what Beacon Labs has been building and I'm impressed by how quickly your team has shipped. I'm writing to ask for a favor — we're deep in a transformation toward AI-assisted engineering and I'm connecting with a few engineering leaders at companies who are ahead of us on this journey.
>
> Would you be open to a short, informal conversation with me and a couple of folks from my team? No pressure if the timing isn't right.
>
> thanks in advance, /John

**What makes this work:**
- Opens with shared context (conference) before the ask
- Genuine specific compliment — names what he noticed, not generic flattery
- Names the ask as a favor — honest and disarming
- States the mission so the ask has weight
- Closes with an out
- Sign-off is personal, not corporate

## Example: Text / iMessage — Warm Outreach to a Former Colleague

> **INSTRUCTIONS:** Paste a real text message you've sent to someone you know personally.

**Context:** Reaching out to a former colleague now at a company relevant to current work.

> Hey Marcus, this is John from Zenith. It's been way too long — hope things are going well with you and the family. I wanted to ask a favor. We're going all in on AI agents at Acme and I'd love to get 30 minutes with you to hear how your team at Influx is thinking about it. Even an informal chat would be super valuable. No pressure if it's not a good time. thanks, /John

**What makes this work:**
- Opens with identity reminder ("this is John from Zenith") since it's a text
- Personal touch before the ask
- Names the favor explicitly
- Humble, specific ask
- Out clause
- Short sign-off with personality

## Rules When Writing For Me

> **INSTRUCTIONS:** List your hard rules. These are applied to every command.

1. Always offer two versions when drafting messages: longer (full context, warmer) and shorter (tighter, faster read)
2. Personal connections go first — before the ask, always
3. Frame asks as favors
4. End with an out: "No pressure if it's not the right timing"
5. Sign-off: `thanks in advance, /John` (or just `/John` for shorter notes)
6. **No em dashes. Ever.** Use a colon, comma, or rewrite the sentence.
7. No AI vocabulary. No corporate tone.
8. If the text is in another language, work in that language — do not translate.
